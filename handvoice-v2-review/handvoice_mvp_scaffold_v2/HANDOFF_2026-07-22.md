# HandVoice Stage 2 Handoff — 22 July 2026

## 1. Executive status

HandVoice is now a working, staff-assisted, non-diagnostic smartphone prototype for measuring how simultaneous right-hand finger tapping and repeated `/pa-ta-ka/` affect motor and speech rhythm in older adults. Its competition differentiator is one synchronized capture that produces an aligned hand–speech timeline, within-person dual-task contrasts and transparent recording-quality evidence.

The engineering MVP and a professionally formatted one-page Nokia proposal are complete. The project is **not yet submission-ready or clinically validated**. The critical path has shifted from adding software features to validating the care workflow, confirming the competition rules, collecting real device/QC evidence and producing the three-minute video.

**Current assessment:** confidence **6/10**; risk **7/10**. The largest risk is the absence of independently validated ageing-care workflow evidence, not the code.

## 2. Frozen project direction

### The problem

Community rehabilitation and allied-health staff often observe hand dexterity, speech rhythm and multitasking separately. Everyday activities can load movement and speech at the same time, but separate observations have no shared clock and are difficult to compare consistently as ageing caseloads grow.

### The solution

A trained staff member uses one smartphone to guide an older adult through three fixed 15-second tasks:

1. Right index–thumb tapping alone (`T01`).
2. Repeated `/pa-ta-ka/` alone (`T02`).
3. Tapping and `/pa-ta-ka/` simultaneously with equal priority (`T03`).

MediaPipe estimates 21 hand landmarks locally. Deterministic audio processing and task logic place hand and speech events on a shared timeline. The system reports within-session contrasts and whether capture quality was accepted, should be retried or needs review.

### Claim boundary

HandVoice measures task performance. It does **not** diagnose, screen for or predict Parkinson’s disease; estimate disease severity; recommend treatment; or make autonomous clinical decisions. Speech fine structure, temporal coupling and speech dual-task outputs remain exploratory until validated against licensed or consented reference annotations. The fixed task order supports within-session comparison, not causal claims about interference.

### AI boundary

The Stage 2 AI component is local computer vision through MediaPipe. Audio analysis, quality decisions and dual-task calculations are deterministic and reproducible. No LLM or agent may change thresholds, accept recordings, alter measurements, infer disease or provide clinical advice. An agentic retry coach remains Stage 3-only and ships only if it beats fixed guidance templates on a golden evaluation set.

## 5. Open gates before submission

### A. Competition rules

Obtain dated primary-source confirmation from the current Nokia email or portal for:

- the Stage 2 deadline;
- the submission portal and filename convention;
- solo/team eligibility;
- video format, upload limit and naming;
- Stage 3 dates, which currently conflict across sources.

No deadline or eligibility assumption should appear in final material until this is resolved.

### B. Problem and workflow validation

Complete at least three interviews with rehabilitation, community-care or allied-health professionals and at least two with older adults, caregivers or credible proxies. Do not collect recordings, diagnoses, health histories or identifiable clinical data during discovery.

The problem gate passes only if at least two professionals independently confirm the same recurring workflow need and can name a concrete, non-diagnostic action enabled by the result. If that does not happen, remove the care-workflow claim or reposition HandVoice as research instrumentation.

### C. Clinical and speech protocol review

A clinician and speech-language professional must decide task wording, repetitions, stop criteria and reference annotation procedure. If this review cannot be obtained, retain the current protocol but remove any suggestion of clinical screening, MDS-UPDRS alignment or established `/pa-ta-ka/` sequence-effect comparability.

### D. Real engineering evidence

- Build a 30-media golden set using licensed or explicitly consented non-clinical material.
- Demonstrate 100% expected quality-decision matching on the golden set.
- Test three representative physical phones; otherwise narrow the supported-device claim.
- Complete five demo runs per device.
- Measure p95 recording-to-report latency, targeting ≤10 seconds; >20 seconds fails the demo gate.
- If known-good capture rejection exceeds 20%, restrict supported devices or use the fallback recording.
- Validate DDK segmentation to F1 ≥0.90 at 20 ms and timing MAE ≤15 ms, or keep all DDK fine-structure outputs exploratory and out of impact claims.

### E. Governance and budget evidence

- Obtain ethics/privacy/security approval before recording real participants.
- Collect dated rates or quotes supporting every SGD 13,200 budget line.
- Produce a bottom-up beneficiary estimate from confirmed site capacity and workflow frequency, not a top-down ageing-population number.

## 7. Do not build next

Do not add disease classifiers, Parkinson’s risk scores, sustained vowels, gait, rest tremor, counting, left-hand tasks, longitudinal monitoring, EHR/FHIR integration, native mobile redevelopment, cloud LLMs, autonomous agents, microservices or public platform scaling.

The uncomfortable but necessary conclusion is that more AI now would make the entry weaker, not stronger. The judges can already see a credible technical prototype. What they cannot yet see is independent evidence that the proposed workflow solves a recurring older-adult care problem and produces an actionable, responsibly governed result.
